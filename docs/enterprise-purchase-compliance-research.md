# Enterprise Purchase Compliance Research: 10,000+ Employee Organizations

## Executive Summary

Large employers (10,000+ employees) follow sophisticated purchase compliance frameworks driven by regulatory requirements, fiduciary responsibilities, and operational efficiency needs. This research outlines key compliance practices relevant to SiriusB iQ's value proposition.

---

## 1. Regulatory Compliance Frameworks

### Federal Compliance Requirements

#### **ERISA (Employee Retirement Income Security Act)**
- **Scope**: Governs employee benefit plans including health insurance
- **Key Requirements**:
  - Fiduciary duty to act in participants' best interests
  - Duty of prudence in selecting and monitoring service providers
  - Duty of loyalty (avoiding conflicts of interest)
  - Required documentation of decision-making process
- **Compliance Actions**:
  - Annual vendor performance reviews
  - Fee benchmarking and disclosure
  - RFP processes every 3-5 years
  - Documentation of cost reasonableness

#### **Affordable Care Act (ACA)**
- **Reporting Requirements**:
  - Forms 1094-C and 1095-C for employer coverage
  - MLR (Medical Loss Ratio) rebate tracking
  - Out-of-pocket maximum compliance
- **Compliance Monitoring**:
  - Minimum essential coverage verification
  - Affordability calculations
  - Dependent coverage to age 26

#### **HIPAA Privacy and Security**
- **Business Associate Agreements (BAAs)**
- **Vendor security assessments**
- **Data breach notification protocols**

#### **Sarbanes-Oxley (SOX) - For Public Companies**
- **Internal controls over financial reporting**
- **Vendor payment verification and reconciliation**
- **Audit trail requirements**

## 3. Standard Purchase Compliance Policies

### Vendor Selection and Contracting

#### **RFP/RFQ Processes**
- **Mandatory for contracts >$500K-$1M annually**
- **Requirements**:
  - Minimum 3 competitive bids
  - Standardized evaluation criteria
  - Pricing transparency and fee disclosure
  - Reference checks and due diligence
  - Board or committee approval for large contracts

#### **Contract Review Requirements**
- Legal review for all service agreements
- Compliance review for regulatory adherence
- CFO approval for multi-year commitments
- Risk assessment for data security

#### **Master Service Agreements (MSAs)**
- Preferred vendor programs
- Volume-based pricing tiers
- Service level agreements (SLAs)
- Termination clauses and transition provisions

---

### Payment and Invoice Verification

#### **Three-Way Match Process**
- **Purchase Order** ↔ **Goods Receipt** ↔ **Invoice**
- Automated matching in ERP systems
- Exception handling workflows
- Approval hierarchies based on dollar thresholds

#### **Invoice Audit Requirements**
- Contract rate verification
- Headcount/eligibility reconciliation
- Service delivery confirmation
- Sales tax and fee validation

#### **Payment Terms and Controls**
- Net 30/45/60 day terms
- Early payment discounts
- Segregation of duties (AP vs. Approver)
- Dual signature requirements for large payments

- Claims payment accuracy testing

## 9. Common Compliance Challenges

### Documented Pain Points

1. **Vendor Billing Errors**
   - 10-20% error rate in benefits invoices (industry estimates)
   - Overcharges often undetected without manual review
   - Retroactive adjustments and reconciliation delays

2. **Contract vs. Actual Pricing Discrepancies**
   - Rate increases without proper notification
   - Hidden fees and surcharges
   - Rebate pass-through verification

3. **Data Integrity Issues**
   - Eligibility file errors (adds/terms/changes)
   - Duplicate coverage scenarios
   - Dependent verification gaps

4. **Documentation and Audit Trails**
   - Decentralized records across departments
   - Lack of real-time visibility
   - Manual reconciliation processes

5. **Regulatory Change Management**
   - Keeping pace with new requirements (CAA, NSA, etc.)
   - Multi-state compliance complexity
   - Vendor readiness for new rules
